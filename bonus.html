Nightingale

The common nightingale, rufous nightingale or simply nightingale (Luscinia megarhynchos), is a small passerine bird best known for its powerful and beautiful song.

  • Nightingales sing even more loudly in urban or near-urban environments, in order to overcome the background noise.
  • The most characteristic feature of the song is a loud whistling crescendo, absent from the song of thrush nightingale. It has a frog-like alarm call.
  • It is not found naturally in the Americas.
